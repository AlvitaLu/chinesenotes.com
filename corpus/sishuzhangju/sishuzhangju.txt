Collected Commentaries on the Four Books by Zhu Xi 朱熹《四書章句集注》

Collected Commentaries on the Four Books《四書章句集注》 is a commentary on the The Four Books of Neo-Confucianism, as referred to by Zhu Xi, namely the Great Learning 《大學》, Doctrine of the Mean 《中庸》, Analects of Confucius 《論語》, and Mencius 《孟子》, by influential Neo-Confucian philosopher Zhu Xi 朱熹 (1130—1200). Zhu Xi is written Chu Hsi in the Wade-Giles Romanization system.

The first and second of the Four Books, Great Learning 《大学》, Doctrine of the Mean 《中庸》, are included in the Classic of Rites 《禮記》. Besides elevating the status of the Great Learning and the Doctrine of the Mean, Zhu Xi also rearranged the texts. (Chan, 1969, p. 85, 97 footnotes) Chan's English translation of Great Learning and the Doctrine of the Mean include many notes from Zhu Xi. (Chan, 1969, pp. 85-114)
